# Topic B — Post-meeting feature parity: what the bot incumbents ship, what's actually used, what to steal

## 1. Vendor-by-vendor snapshot (post-meeting features only)

**Otter.ai** — summaries with auto-extracted/assigned action items; **AI Chat** (cross-meeting natural-language Q&A: "list all action items from last week's calls"); **AI Channels** (meetings + action items + chat grouped by project/series); speaker diarization with talk-time. In April 2026 Otter pivoted to a "Conversational Knowledge Engine": an **expanded MCP server**, AI Chat, and **Otter for Desktop (Mac/Windows) that captures any app's audio bot-free** ([Otter blog](https://otter.ai/blog/otter-ai-evolves-from-ai-notetaker-to-create-100b-enterprise-conversational-knowledge-engine-market), Apr 2026; [otter.ai](https://otter.ai/)).

**Fireflies.ai** — **AskFred** cross-meeting Q&A with timestamped citations; **topic trackers** (auto-surface competitor/budget/feature-request mentions across all meetings); talk-time, sentiment, conversation intelligence; CRM auto-logging (Salesforce/HubSpot; Affinity via Zapier); **Soundbites** clips + shareable Playlists ([Soundbites guide](https://guide.fireflies.ai/articles/8029743344-Learn+About+Soundbites+(Meeting+Clips+&+Highlights))); auto follow-up email drafting via AI Skills ([fireflies.ai](https://fireflies.ai/)). Fireflies shipped **the first AI-meeting MCP connector for Claude** (Web/Desktop/Mobile/Code) ([Fireflies blog](https://fireflies.ai/blog/fireflies-claude/), [MCP server post](https://fireflies.ai/blog/fireflies-mcp-server/)).

**Fathom** — frictionless free tier (G2 ~5.0/5 from 6,200+ reviews — the best-loved product in the category, per [summarizemeeting G2 comparison](https://summarizemeeting.com/en/comparison/g2-ratings-meeting-tools)); one-click live "highlight" that lands pre-formatted in Slack/Notion/Asana; **Ask Fathom** cross-library Q&A ("turns passive recordings into an active knowledge base" — [aiunpacker 90-day review](https://aiunpacker.com/blog/fathom-ai-note-taker-honest-review-after-90-days-of-use/)); automatic Salesforce/HubSpot sync; Business tier adds CRM field sync, coaching metrics, talk-time scorecards ([toolvetting](https://www.toolvetting.com/fathom-ai-review/)).

**Circleback** — best-in-class **action-item extraction** ("I'll send you the deck by Friday" → assigned task with deadline — its most praised feature, [dupple review](https://dupple.com/tools/circleback)); a native **automation builder**: post-meeting rules routed by attendee, meeting size ("number of invitees" condition added 2026), pushing to Linear/Notion/Slack/CRM without Zapier ([circleback.ai/releases](https://circleback.ai/releases)).

**tl;dv** — clips/reels for sharing 2-minute segments; **multi-meeting AI reports** (weekly digest: top topics, objections, feature-request backlog, sentiment trends across hundreds of calls); sales-playbook scoring; 5,000+ integrations; notably added a **botless recording mode** "for sensitive negotiations" ([spilno tl;dv review](https://spilnoagency.com.ua/en/instructions-us/overview-of-the-tldv-service), [thebusinessdive 18-month review](https://thebusinessdive.com/tldv-review)).

**Read.ai** — engagement/sentiment "Read Score", Speaker Coach (pace, filler words, clarity/inclusion/impact) ([Read support](https://support.read.ai/hc/en-us/articles/4406653674003-About-Sentiment-Engagement-and-the-Read-Score)). Widely criticized: sentiment is "directional data, not gospel," misreads expressions, and Read's aggressive auto-join made it the poster child of bot backlash — Oxford University blocked Read.ai/Fireflies/Sembly in Aug 2025 ([Oxford InfoSec](https://www.infosec.ox.ac.uk/article/are-your-online-meetings-safe-from-third-party-ai-bots), [voicetypingtools review](https://www.voicetypingtools.com/tools/read-ai)).

**Granola (the standout newer one)** — bot-free, Mac-native, sparse human notes AI-expanded post-meeting; $1.5B valuation Mar 2026; repositioned as an **enterprise "AI context layer" with personal + enterprise APIs feeding Claude/ChatGPT** ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/granola-raises-125m-hits-1-5b-valuation-as-it-expands-from-meeting-notetaker-to-enterprise-ai-app/), Mar 25 2026). Ranked **#1 for investor meetings** by Value Add VC precisely because no bot preserves founder candor ([valueaddvc.com](https://valueaddvc.com/blog/top-6-ai-notetaking-tools-for-investor-meetings-granola-otter-fireflies-and-more-ranked), May 2026).

## 2. Valued vs shelfware (review evidence)

**Consistently valued:** clear summaries + transcripts, action items with owners, cross-meeting search, CRM auto-logging. G2 analysis of 1,000+ reviews: users most praise "full transcripts and summaries, along with action items and responsible parties" ([G2 Learn](https://learn.g2.com/best-ai-meeting-assistants)). Ask-Fathom-style cross-library Q&A is repeatedly called the feature "you wonder how you worked without."

**Shelfware / gimmick territory:** sentiment & engagement scoring (Read.ai's core) — imprecise with sarcasm, culture, monotone speakers; "more than you need" for most users ([tooldirectory comparison](https://tooldirectory.ai/blog/ai-notetakers-2026-otter-fireflies-granola-fathom-read), Jun 4 2026). Sales coaching/playbook scoring is sales-team-specific. Basic AI summaries themselves are now commodity — "the summary is free now" (platform built-ins do it).

**VC-specific signal:** VC-focused roundups emphasize (a) bot-free candor — "a founder pitching... doesn't necessarily want a third-party AI bot" ([meetingnotes.com](https://meetingnotes.com/blog/best-ai-meeting-notetakers-for-venture-capital-firms), Apr 21 2026); (b) CRM push to deal records — **Affinity's own native Notetaker** syncs notes to deal/person/company records, with TELUS Global Ventures reporting **1–2.5 hrs saved/person/week and 60% reduction in missing CRM data** ([affinity.co](https://www.affinity.co/guides/vc-ai-tools)); (c) searchable transcript database across deal flow for multi-partner teams. Follow-up email automation and engagement analytics are conspicuously absent from VC rankings.

**Bot backlash context (validates NO-BOT):** Gartner predicted 40% of enterprises restricting/banning third-party meeting bots by 2025; 84% of professionals admit altering how they speak when an AI notetaker is visible ([UMEVO](https://www.umevo.ai/blogs/ume-all-posts/the-bot-backlash-why-clients-refuse-meetings-with-ai-notetaker-bots), [Basil AI](https://basilai.app/articles/2026-05-11-ai-meeting-bots-chilling-effect-self-censorship-workplace-speech-on-device-privacy.html)).

## 3. Ranked "steal this" list (for a context-package-into-Claude tool)

Given LLM post-processing is ~free for us, features split into "trivial given a good transcript" vs "requires capture-time data":

1. **Action items with owner + deadline (Circleback-grade)** — trivial via Claude skill; *requires reliable per-speaker attribution* so "I'll send the deck" binds to a person. Capture need: diarization + speaker→identity mapping (calendar attendees).
2. **CRM auto-push with deal/contact matching** (Affinity-parity, into Sevanta) — highest VC ROI per Affinity's own numbers. Mostly exists already via the `process-meeting` skill; the parity gap is **Circleback-style routing rules** (meeting type, attendee domain, invitee count → which skill runs). Capture need: calendar metadata (title, attendees, organizer) bundled in the context package.
3. **Cross-meeting search / ask-AI** (AskFred / Ask Fathom / Otter AI Chat) — for incumbents this is infrastructure; for us it's a Claude skill over an indexed folder of context packages. Capture need: consistent package schema + stable meeting IDs + per-utterance timestamps for citation.
4. **Sparse human notes merged with transcript (Granola's pattern)** — the single most VC-loved interaction model; partner jots 5 bullets, AI expands with transcript evidence. Capture need: a lightweight in-meeting note field, timestamped against the recording.
5. **Multi-meeting digests** (tl;dv weekly reports) — weekly deal-flow digest, recurring-founder-meeting deltas ("what changed since last call") — trivial with Claude + scheduled skill over packages.
6. **Topic trackers** (Fireflies) — auto-flag valuation mentions, competitor names, round terms across all meetings; trivial as a post-pass once corpus exists.
7. **Follow-up email draft** — trivial LLM; modest VC value (thank-you/next-steps to founders). Capture need: attendee emails.
8. **Talk-time / monologue analytics** — moderate VC value (founder vs investor talk ratio is a genuine pitch-quality signal). **Cannot be reconstructed post-hoc: requires per-speaker timestamps at record time.** Note: bot tools get speaker names from platform participant metadata; a no-bot recorder must diarize acoustically and map via mic-channel-vs-system-audio separation + enrollment/calendar heuristics.
9. **Snippet/clip sharing** (Soundbites, tl;dv clips) — low value for confidential VC meetings (sharing audio of an invisible recording is a trust landmine); but keep **word-level timestamps** anyway — cheap at capture, enables citations and clips later.
10. **Skip entirely:** sentiment/engagement scoring, speaker coaching, playbook adherence — the most criticized, least VC-relevant tier.

**Capture-time data checklist implied by the above:** per-speaker diarization with timestamps; separate mic vs system-audio channels (free speaker separation for the local user); word-level timestamps; calendar event metadata (title, attendees, emails, recurrence); meeting platform + invitee count; in-meeting manual notes with timestamps; retained audio (policy-configurable).
