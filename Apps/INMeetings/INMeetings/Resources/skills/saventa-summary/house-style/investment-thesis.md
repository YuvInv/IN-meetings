# IN Venture — Investment Thesis

## Fund Profile

- **Stage**: Seed and Series A
- **Check size**: Up to $5M
- **Geography**: Israel-headquartered fund, global dealflow
- **Focus**: B2B SaaS — developer tools, infrastructure, vertical AI

## Sweet Spot

Companies that fit best:
- B2B SaaS with clear developer or enterprise buyer
- Dev tools, cloud infrastructure, data infrastructure, DevOps/platform engineering
- Vertical AI — AI applied to specific industry workflows (not generic horizontal AI)
- Strong technical founding team (ideally Israeli ecosystem — 8200, Talpiot, repeat founders)
- Pre-revenue to early revenue at Seed; $1M+ ARR trajectory at Series A
- Capital-efficient — not burning through cash without milestones

## Evaluation Framework

### Team (1-5 rating)
- 5: Repeat founders with domain expertise + technical depth + complementary skills
- 4: Strong technical founders with relevant industry experience
- 3: Good technical team but missing domain expertise or business co-founder
- 2: Single founder or team gaps in critical areas
- 1: No clear technical advantage or relevant experience

### Tech Depth
- **Deep tech**: Novel algorithms, patents, significant R&D moat (12+ months to replicate)
- **Semi-deep**: Meaningful technical differentiation but not a pure research play
- **Not deep**: Execution play — differentiation is in GTM, UX, or speed, not tech

### Market Assessment
- TAM should be $1B+ for Series A; $500M+ acceptable for Seed
- Clear buyer persona and pain point
- Existing budget line item (replacing something) is stronger than creating new category
- Regulation as moat is interesting; regulation as barrier is a risk

### Alignment Scoring
- **Strong fit**: Core thesis match (B2B SaaS + dev tools/infra/vertical AI + Israel + right stage)
- **Moderate fit**: Adjacent to thesis (e.g., B2B SaaS but horizontal, or right space but too early/late)
- **Weak fit**: Outside thesis (consumer, hardware, biotech, etc.)

## Red Flags
- No technical co-founder in a tech company
- TAM claims that don't survive basic math
- "AI" that's really just an API wrapper with a prompt
- Burn rate inconsistent with stage and funding
- Founder can't articulate differentiation vs. competitors
- No customer conversations / pure vision play at Series A

## Green Flags
- Repeat founders from Israeli unicorns
- Open-source traction / developer community adoption
- Design partners or LOIs from recognizable enterprises
- Capital efficiency — doing more with less
- Clear "why now" — market timing story that holds up
